Perfect! I've analyzed your Godot game project. Here's a comprehensive list of all components organized by category:

## **PLAYER & CHARACTER**
- **Player** – Your main character with movement controls, jumping (400 units/sec), damage system, knockback, and invincibility frames

## **ENEMIES**
- **Evil Sorbet** – Patrolling enemy that moves between waypoints and deals 1 damage on contact (with cooldown)

## **HAZARDS & OBSTACLES**
- **Spikes** – Stationary hazard that damages the player on contact
- **Locked Block** – Obstacle that requires collecting keys to unlock; removes collision/sprite once unlocked

## **COLLECTIBLES**
- **Health Pickup** – Restores 1 health when collected (won't pick up at max health)
- **Key (Fuckass Key)** – Collectible key used to unlock locked blocks

## **CHECKPOINT & PROGRESSION**
- **Checkpoint** – Save point that stores player spawn position when touched
- **Win Trigger** – Goal area that shows the win screen when reached

## **UI & HUD**
- **Ice Cream Health HUD** – Displays current health (0-4) using ice cream sprite states
- **Controls Label Settings** – Label styling resource for UI

## **MENUS & SCREENS**
- **Main Menu V1 & V2** – Two versions of the main menu with Play, Controls, and Credits screens
- **Death Screen** – Game over screen with Retry button
- **Win Screen** – Victory screen with Play Again button

## **GLOBAL SYSTEMS**
- **Global Manager** – Central state controller tracking player health, keys, score, current level, and checkpoint positions
- **Main Game Manager** – Game loop that spawns the player and manages input/menu visibility

## **SPRITES**
Health display (Max/3/2/1 Health), Player sprite, Enemy sprite, Collectibles (Health Pickup, Key), Hazards (Spike), Environment (Tileset, Grass, Locked Block tiles), Menu backgrounds, and Win sprite.

The game flow is: **Menu → Level Start → Collect keys/health → Avoid hazards → Reach goal → Win Screen → Restart**
